# Aerodrone Hardware Shopping List

Goal: build a quadrotor that can mirror the CyberPhysics Aerostack2 navigation
simulation: GPS/odom, downward OAK-1 RGB for WildNav, and OAK-1 MegaDepth
relative depth for DemNav.

Important compatibility note

- The user-requested target is Raspberry Pi 5 plus a Blue Robotics flight
  controller hat. Blue Robotics currently sells the Navigator as a Raspberry Pi
  4 flight-controller expansion board. Before purchasing for a Pi 5 build,
  confirm Pi 5 mechanical/header/software compatibility with Blue Robotics or
  buy a Raspberry Pi 4 specifically for the Navigator. The rest of this list
  assumes the Navigator-style I/O stack: PWM motor outputs, SBUS RC input,
  onboard IMU/compass/barometer, and BlueOS/ArduPilot setup.

Quick-buy baseline

- 1 x 450 mm to 500 mm quadcopter frame kit with landing gear and enough top-deck
  room for Raspberry Pi, Navigator, battery, and the downward OAK-1. Recommended
  quick route: Holybro X500 V2 or equivalent ArduPilot-friendly quad frame.
- 4 x matched brushless motors appropriate for the frame, commonly 2216-920 kV
  or similar for a 450/500 mm 4S build.
- 4 x PWM-capable ESCs, 30 A to 40 A, compatible with the selected motors and
  props. The Navigator has PWM outputs, so avoid ESCs that require a flight-
  controller protocol the stack cannot drive.
- 2 x full prop sets, clockwise and counter-clockwise, sized for the motor/frame
  combination. Buy extra props for first-flight tuning.
- 1 x power distribution board or harness with XT60/XT90 main connector.
- 2 x 4S LiPo batteries, 4000 mAh to 6000 mAh, with a discharge rating suitable
  for the motor/prop setup.
- 1 x LiPo balance charger and fire-safe charging bag.
- 1 x Raspberry Pi 5, preferably 8 GB or 16 GB.
- 1 x Raspberry Pi 5 active cooler.
- 1 x high-endurance 128 GB or larger microSD card, or an M.2/NVMe HAT if the
  software image and vibration budget support it.
- 1 x 5 V/5 A to 5 V/6 A regulated power supply for the Raspberry Pi/flight
  controller stack, fed from the main battery.
- 1 x Blue Robotics Navigator Flight Controller kit, plus the vendor-recommended
  Power Sense Module and 5 V 6 A supply option.
- 1 x GPS + compass module with JST-GH cabling. Recommended: u-blox M9N/M10N or
  Here3+/Here4-class module supported by ArduPilot.
- 1 x RC transmitter and SBUS/ELRS receiver for manual control and failsafe.
- 1 x telemetry link for setup and field debugging. Use USB/Ethernet/Wi-Fi on the
  bench and a 915 MHz/868 MHz telemetry radio or equivalent for the field.
- 1 x downward-facing Luxonis OAK-1 camera for both WildNav imagery and DemNav
  depth input. Recommended: OAK-1 USB, fixed-focus or wide-FOV variant, mounted
  nadir-facing. The CyberPhysics OAK-1 node runs MegaDepth on the camera's
  Myriad X and publishes relative depth to /oak1/relative_depth.
- 1 x USB 3.0 screw-locking cable for OAK-1, plus strain relief. If cable
  robustness and remote power are more important than weight and wiring
  simplicity, use OAK-1 PoE with an onboard PoE injector/switch and verify the
  power budget before flight.
- 1 x vibration-isolated OAK-1 mount with an adjustable nadir pitch plate and
  clear view below the frame.
- 1 x downward rangefinder for landing and altitude sanity checks. Recommended:
  Benewake TFmini-S/TF-Luna for budget builds, or LightWare SF11/C for higher
  confidence. Keep this even with OAK-1 because MegaDepth is relative, not a
  direct metric depth sensor.
- 1 x vibration isolation kit for flight controller and camera deck.
- 1 x prop guards for indoor/first tests, if payload clearance allows.
- 1 x small hardware kit: M2.5/M3 screws, nylon lock nuts, standoffs, threadlocker,
  battery straps, foam tape, zip ties, cable sleeving, and heat-shrink tubing.
- 1 x field safety kit: LiPo voltage checker, spare props, spare arms, spare ESC,
  spare motor, smoke stopper, multimeter, and first-flight landing pad.

Recommended assembly order

1. Assemble frame, motors, ESCs, and power distribution with props removed.
2. Mount the Raspberry Pi/Navigator stack near the center of gravity and wire
   PWM outputs, power sense, GPS/compass, RC receiver, and telemetry.
3. Verify ArduPilot motor order, RC failsafe, battery monitor, compass, and IMU
   calibration before installing propellers.
4. Mount the OAK-1 rigidly downward and verify the MegaDepth pipeline:
   - WildNav should consume /oak1/image_highres.
   - DemNav should consume /oak1/relative_depth.
   - Camera calibration should come from /oak1/camera_info or
     /oak1/image_highres/camera_info, depending on the consumer.
5. Run tethered or prop-off bench tests until GPS, odom, OAK-1 RGB,
   MegaDepth relative depth, rangefinder altitude, and camera calibration topics
   are stable.
6. Perform manual hover and failsafe tests before enabling any autonomous
   waypoint, DemNav, or WildNav behavior.
