# Lunar Rover Hardware Shopping List

Goal: build a CyberPhysics lunar rover development platform around Space ROS,
the existing `applications/spaceros` image, and a six-wheel rover that can be
validated in simulation before a terrestrial regolith testbed run.

Important scope note

- This is a terrestrial prototype and software validation platform, not a
  flight-ready lunar vehicle. Actual lunar hardware needs mission-specific
  radiation, vacuum, thermal, launch-load, contamination-control, reliability,
  and planetary-protection engineering that cannot be satisfied by a quick COTS
  shopping list.
- The simulation is intentionally patterned after NASA lunar rover use cases:
  crater-rim traverse, south-pole illumination/shadow, power/thermal management,
  regolith slip, and supervised autonomous waypoint operations.

Quick-buy baseline

- 1 x six-wheel rover chassis with rocker-bogie or articulated suspension,
  skid-steer/differential-drive kinematics, and at least 20 kg payload capacity.
- 6 x high-torque geared brushless or brushed DC wheel motors sized for low
  speed, high traction, and outdoor regolith/sand analog testing.
- 6 x wheel encoders or integrated smart motor controllers with velocity
  feedback.
- 6 x compliant grousers/cleated wheels or replaceable tread wheels for sand,
  gravel, and regolith simulant.
- 2 x motor controller banks with current sensing and ROS 2-compatible control
  interfaces, such as CAN, RS-485, or USB.
- 1 x onboard compute module capable of running Space ROS and ROS 2 Humble,
  preferably an x86-64 rugged mini PC or Jetson-class computer with 16 GB RAM.
- 1 x safety microcontroller or watchdog board for motor inhibit, e-stop,
  heartbeat monitoring, and power sequencing.
- 1 x 9-axis IMU with temperature compensation.
- 1 x wheel odometry interface wired into the motor/encoder stack.
- 1 x forward stereo camera or depth camera for hazard perception in the
  terrestrial prototype.
- 1 x mast or front hazard camera pair for low-sun shadow and obstacle tests.
- 1 x downward terrain camera for slip/soil visual odometry experiments.
- 1 x 2D or 3D lidar for lab and field validation if the test environment is
  not camera-only.
- 1 x GNSS receiver for Earth outdoor testing only; disable or replace with
  simulated/localization inputs for lunar mission validation.
- 1 x 24 V or 28 V battery pack with BMS, service disconnect, fuse block, and
  current/voltage telemetry.
- 1 x solar panel simulator or removable solar panel for power-management
  tests; size it as a payload input, not as the primary field safety power.
- 1 x thermal sensor set for electronics bay, battery, motor drivers, and
  external body temperature.
- 1 x onboard Ethernet switch for cameras, lidar, and payload compute.
- 1 x Wi-Fi 6, private LTE, or long-range Ethernet radio for supervised field
  operations.
- 1 x operator laptop with Docker, Space ROS image builds, RViz/Foxglove, and
  manual teleoperation tools.
- 1 x gamepad or hardware joystick for manual recovery.
- 1 x physical e-stop and remote e-stop receiver.
- 1 x field kit: spare wheels, spare motor controller, fuses, cabling, crimp
  tools, fasteners, threadlocker, multimeter, battery-safe storage, and PPE.
- 1 x terrain testbed or field area with sand/regolith simulant, slopes,
  shadows, rocks, and clearly marked safety boundaries.

Software stack

- Space ROS / ROS 2 Humble in `applications/spaceros`.
- Space ROS Curiosity rover demo for baseline rover workflows.
- CyberPhysics `lunar_rover_sim.py` for deterministic south-pole terrain,
  slip, illumination, thermal, battery, IMU, wheel-state, and odometry topics.
- CyberPhysics `lunar_waypoint_nav.py` for autonomous waypoint traverse and
  terrain-aware speed limiting.
- Optional RViz or Foxglove for telemetry inspection.
