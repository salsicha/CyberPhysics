# RACECAR Neo Hardware Shopping List

Goal: build a physical 1/10-scale RACECAR Neo that can match the simulated
CyberPhysics pipeline: forward RGB camera -> Depth Anything -> nvblox/Nav2,
with lidar, IMU, odometry, and Ackermann command output.

Quick-buy baseline

- 1 x 1/10 4WD RC car platform, preferably a Traxxas Slash 4x4, Traxxas Rally,
  or equivalent 1/10 brushless short-course/rally chassis with spare parts
  readily available.
- 1 x NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano Super Developer Kit, 8 GB.
- 1 x 128 GB or larger NVMe SSD for the Jetson, plus a compatible M.2 carrier
  if the selected Jetson carrier does not expose NVMe directly.
- 1 x regulated Jetson power rail from the drive battery, sized for the carrier
  board input. Use a 19 V/5 A buck-boost module for the developer kit barrel
  input, or the carrier vendor's recommended automotive DC input.
- 1 x forward-facing RGB-D camera. Recommended: RealSense D455 for mobile/outdoor
  range, or RealSense D435i for shorter indoor testing with built-in IMU.
  A plain USB global-shutter RGB camera is enough for the Depth Anything path,
  but RGB-D gives a useful fallback depth topic.
- 1 x USB 3.0 short locking cable for the camera, plus strain relief.
- 1 x 2D lidar for /scan. Recommended budget option: Slamtec RPLIDAR C1/S2/A2.
  Recommended robust option: Hokuyo UST-10LX or UST-20LX.
- 1 x IMU if the selected camera IMU is not used for vehicle state estimation.
  Recommended: BNO085/BNO086 breakout, WitMotion WT901C, or equivalent ROS-
  friendly 6/9-axis IMU.
- 1 x VESC 6-class motor controller, or equivalent VESC-compatible ESC with
  UART/USB/CAN telemetry.
- 1 x steering servo interface. Use the vehicle's included steering servo if it
  is reliable; otherwise buy a 20 kg-cm or stronger metal-gear waterproof servo.
- 1 x microcontroller bridge for servo/PWM, encoder, and safety I/O. Recommended:
  Teensy 4.1, Arduino Nano 33 IoT, or similar board with ROS 2 micro-ROS support.
- 1 x wheel speed/odometry kit. Use motor encoder telemetry from the VESC when
  available; otherwise add a magnetic wheel encoder or optical encoder.
- 1 x RC transmitter and receiver for manual override and first-drive testing.
  Recommended: ELRS or Spektrum/Futaba receiver with PWM/SBUS output.
- 1 x physical emergency-stop switch that cuts motor power while leaving compute
  power available for clean logs.
- 1 x main power switch, inline fuse, XT60/XT90 connectors, and power
  distribution board.
- 2 x drive batteries compatible with the chassis and VESC, usually 2S or 3S
  LiPo packs. Buy at least two packs so one can charge while one is installed.
- 1 x balance charger rated for the selected LiPo packs.
- 1 x 5 V/5 A buck converter for USB hubs, microcontroller, and low-voltage
  accessories.
- 1 x powered USB 3 hub if the Jetson carrier does not have enough stable USB
  ports for camera, lidar, microcontroller, and setup peripherals.
- 1 x Wi-Fi 6 or Ethernet bridge. Prefer Ethernet for lab bring-up; use Wi-Fi
  only after the ROS graph is stable.
- 1 x top electronics plate. Use carbon fiber, G10, acrylic, or 3D printed
  standoffs that isolate electronics from chassis vibration.
- 1 x forward camera mount with adjustable pitch and vibration isolation.
- 1 x lidar mast or top-deck mount with a clear 360 degree view.
- 1 x small hardware kit: M2.5/M3 screws, standoffs, heat-set inserts, zip ties,
  cable sleeving, adhesive cable anchors, threadlocker, and Velcro straps.

Recommended assembly order

1. Assemble and test the RC car in manual mode with the stock radio.
2. Install VESC/control electronics and verify throttle/steering with wheels off
   the ground.
3. Add Jetson, camera, lidar, and IMU on a removable top plate.
4. Verify ROS topics before autonomous driving:
   - /camera/color/image_raw
   - /camera/color/camera_info
   - /scan
   - /imu/data_raw
   - /ackermann_cmd
5. Add the emergency stop before enabling Nav2 or autonomous throttle.
