# Airplane Hardware Shopping List

Goal: build a fixed-wing mapping airplane around Raspberry Pi 5, BlueOS,
Navigator, ArduPlane, GPS, and a downward camera that can localize with
satellite maps and DEM matching like the Aerodrone stack.

Important compatibility note

- The requested target is Raspberry Pi 5 plus BlueOS and Navigator if possible.
  BlueOS supports Raspberry Pi 5-class deployments, but the Blue Robotics
  Navigator product line has historically targeted the Raspberry Pi 4 form
  factor. Before purchasing, confirm Pi 5 mechanical/header/software fit with
  Blue Robotics. If that fit is blocked, use a Raspberry Pi 4 for the Navigator
  flight-controller stack and keep the Raspberry Pi 5 as a companion computer.
- This is an ArduPlane fixed-wing aircraft. Do not reuse multicopter tuning,
  failsafe, arming, or mission assumptions from the aerodrone.

Quick-buy baseline

- 1 x fixed-wing mapping airframe with stable low-speed handling, payload bay,
  and clean downward camera view. Good first choices: Hee Wing Ranger T1,
  Skywalker X8, Volantex Ranger EX, or another ArduPilot-proven foam mapping
  platform with a pusher prop and enough payload margin.
- 1 x matched brushless motor, ESC, and propeller set for the selected airframe.
- 1 x spare propeller set and motor mount hardware.
- 4 x to 6 x airframe servos for ailerons, elevator, rudder, flaps, or elevons,
  depending on the airframe.
- 1 x servo power rail or BEC sized for all control-surface servos at stall.
- 1 x Raspberry Pi 5, preferably 8 GB or 16 GB.
- 1 x Raspberry Pi 5 active cooler and high-endurance 128 GB or larger microSD
  card, or a supported NVMe HAT if vibration mounting is solved.
- 1 x Blue Robotics Navigator Flight Controller kit, plus recommended power
  sense and 5 V supply hardware.
- 1 x BlueOS image and ArduPlane firmware configured through BlueOS.
- 1 x digital airspeed sensor and pitot tube. Treat this as mandatory for
  repeatable fixed-wing autonomous flight and wind handling.
- 1 x GPS + compass module supported by ArduPilot, such as u-blox M9N/M10N or
  Here3+/Here4-class hardware.
- 1 x RC transmitter and receiver for manual launch, recovery, and failsafe.
- 1 x telemetry link for field setup and monitoring, such as Wi-Fi/Ethernet on
  the bench plus 915 MHz/868 MHz telemetry radio for the field.
- 1 x downward-facing Luxonis OAK-1 USB or OAK-1 PoE camera mounted nadir.
  Use it for high-resolution WildNav RGB and MegaDepth relative depth for
  DemNav. Pick PoE if cable retention and remote mounting matter more than
  weight and power complexity.
- 1 x vibration-isolated nadir camera mount with a clear view through the belly
  or below the fuselage.
- 1 x USB 3.0 screw-locking cable, or PoE injector/switch for OAK-1 PoE.
- 1 x optional nadir global-shutter camera if fast low-altitude passes cause
  rolling-shutter blur in the OAK-1 RGB stream.
- 1 x lithium battery pack sized for the aircraft motor and avionics load, plus
  a separate avionics BEC if the main ESC BEC is not robust enough.
- 1 x LiPo/Li-ion balance charger, fire-safe charging bag, and battery checker.
- 1 x launch and recovery kit: bungee or hand-launch helper if needed, landing
  markers, prop safety tools, spare control horns, hinges, pushrods, clevises,
  tape, glue, and field repair foam.
- 1 x operator laptop with QGroundControl, Docker, ROS 2 tools, and access to
  the CyberPhysics compose stacks.

Software stack

- BlueOS on the onboard Raspberry Pi.
- ArduPlane on the Navigator.
- MAVROS in the `cyberphysics/aerostack2` image.
- CyberPhysics `airplane_mavros_bridge.py` to mirror MAVROS telemetry into the
  `/plane0/sensor_measurements/*` contract.
- OAK-1 MegaDepth for `/airplane/downward/image_raw`,
  `/airplane/downward/image_highres`, and `/airplane/downward/relative_depth`.
- DemNav consuming GPS, odometry, camera info, and relative depth.
- WildNav consuming downward RGB and satellite tiles, then publishing fused
  `/airplane/navigation/odometry`.
- QGroundControl for ArduPlane setup, calibration, mission upload, manual
  control, and safety monitoring.

ROS 2 topic contract

- `/plane0/sensor_measurements/gps`
- `/plane0/sensor_measurements/odom`
- `/plane0/sensor_measurements/imu`
- `/plane0/sensor_measurements/battery`
- `/airplane/downward/image_raw`
- `/airplane/downward/camera_info`
- `/airplane/downward/image_highres`
- `/airplane/downward/image_highres/camera_info`
- `/airplane/downward/relative_depth`
- `/demnav/odometry`
- `/wildnav/odometry`
- `/airplane/navigation/odometry`

Simulation baseline

- Use ArduPilot Plane SITL with JSBSim for high-fidelity fixed-wing flight
  dynamics and autopilot behavior.
- Use `systems/airplane/scripts/satellite_camera_sim.py` to provide a
  georeferenced nadir RGB/depth stream for DemNav/WildNav when a full
  photorealistic terrain simulator is not available.
- The compose stack uses the ArduPilot dev-base Docker image and keeps the
  cloned simulator source under `cache/ardupilot`, outside tracked files.
