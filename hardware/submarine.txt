# BlueROV2 Submarine Hardware Shopping List

Goal: build a CyberPhysics submarine around Blue Robotics BlueROV2, Raspberry
Pi 5, BlueOS, Navigator, ArduSub, and the `applications/submarine` ROS 2 bridge.

Compatibility note

- The requested platform is Raspberry Pi 5 plus Navigator. BlueOS documentation
  lists Raspberry Pi 5 support, but the BlueROV2 product page and Navigator
  flight-controller documentation still describe the standard stack as Navigator
  plus Raspberry Pi 4. Treat Pi 5 + Navigator as a custom BlueOS integration and
  validate fit, thermal performance, power, camera streaming, and Navigator
  service startup before sealing the electronics enclosure.

Quick-buy baseline

- 1 x Blue Robotics BlueROV2 base package. Choose acrylic for 100 m class work
  or aluminum for deeper operations.
- 1 x Heavy Configuration Upgrade if full six-degree-of-freedom control,
  additional stability, or heavier payloads are required.
- 1 x Raspberry Pi 5, preferably 8 GB or 16 GB.
- 1 x Raspberry Pi 5 active cooler and high-endurance 128 GB or larger microSD
  card, or an NVMe HAT only if the enclosure and BlueOS image support it.
- 1 x Blue Robotics Navigator Flight Controller.
- 1 x BlueOS image for Raspberry Pi 5.
- 1 x ArduSub firmware configured through BlueOS.
- 1 x Fathom tether of the required operating length.
- 1 x Fathom-X or approved topside/tether interface.
- 1 x tether spool sized for the tether length.
- 1 x laptop running Cockpit, QGroundControl, or another MAVLink-capable control
  station.
- 1 x gamepad controller for manual operation and recovery.
- 1 x ROV battery and 1 x compatible charger; buy at least two batteries for
  field operations.
- 1 x Bar30 or appropriate pressure/depth sensor if not included in the selected
  kit.
- 1 x leak sensor connected to the Navigator.
- 2 x or 4 x Lumen lights, depending on inspection-lighting needs.
- 1 x spare T200 thruster if downtime matters.
- 1 x spare propeller set, penetrator blanks, O-rings, silicone grease, and
  enclosure service kit.
- 1 x optional Ping360, imaging sonar, DVL, USBL, or acoustic modem depending on
  navigation and inspection requirements.
- 1 x field kit: vacuum pump/test fittings, dry bag, hand tools, corrosion
  control supplies, multimeter, towels, spare fasteners, cable ties, and labels.

Software stack

- BlueOS on the onboard Raspberry Pi.
- ArduSub on the Navigator.
- MAVROS inside `cyberphysics/submarine`.
- CyberPhysics `bluerov2_bridge.py` for ROS 2 telemetry topics.
- Cockpit or QGroundControl for vehicle setup, manual operation, video, and
  missions.

ROS 2 topic contract

- `/sub0/sensor_measurements/gps`
- `/sub0/sensor_measurements/odom`
- `/sub0/sensor_measurements/imu`
- `/sub0/sensor_measurements/pressure`
- `/sub0/sensor_measurements/battery`
- `/sub0/cmd_vel` only after velocity setpoints are explicitly enabled and
  tested in a controlled test tank.
